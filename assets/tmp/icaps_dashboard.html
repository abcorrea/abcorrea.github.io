Methodology & caveats
Paper and author lists were read from the AAAI OJS ICAPS proceedings archive (volumes 30–35). No paper or PDF was opened — titles and author bylines came from each issue's table-of-contents page only.
  • Citation counts come from the Semantic Scholar Graph API, queried consistently for all 443 papers. Google Scholar has no public API and actively blocks automated queries, so it isn't usable for a reproducible sweep at this scale — Semantic Scholar is the closest widely-used, citable equivalent.
  • Snapshot date: 2026-07-03. Counts will keep rising after this date, especially for 2024–2025 papers.
  • Matching was done by exact and verified fuzzy title match; a small number of records (mostly 2025 papers, plus a few whose Semantic Scholar entry is filed under an arXiv preprint title, e.g. "Width-Based Planning and Active Learning for Atari" for "Is Policy Learning Overrated?") were cross-checked by DOI against DBLP/Crossref before accepting the citation count.
  • 2025's average is naturally low — those papers have had only months, not years, to accumulate citations. Read the year chart as a citation-age effect, not a quality signal.
  • Author rankings sum citations across every paper an author co-authored in this window; a single highly-cited paper with few authors (e.g. PDDLStream) lifts its authors to the top of that ranking even with just one paper.
